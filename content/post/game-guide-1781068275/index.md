# 🍲 **The Ultimate Teyvat Isekai Friendship Guide: Who You Can *Actually* Befriend in Genshin Impact (No Wish-Granting Required!)**

> *Forget overpowered OCs and forced lore-bending—this is your realistic, lore-accurate roadmap to building genuine bonds in Teyvat.*

## 🔍 Summary  
In a charmingly grounded Reddit thread on r/Genshin_Impact, players debated a beloved fantasy: *“You get isekai’d to Teyvat—who do you think you can be close with *realistically*?”* Rather than defaulting to Vision-holders or Archons, the community gravitated toward characters whose roles, personalities, and daily lives make organic, low-stakes friendship not just plausible—but *probable*. The standout consensus? **Xiangling**, the fiery, food-obsessed 14-year-old chef of Wanmin Restaurant in Liyue Harbor. Her warm demeanor, accessible location, non-combat-centric lifestyle, and canonically inclusive attitude toward strangers (see: her open-door policy for taste-testers and “spice enthusiasts”) make her the gold standard for *achievable* Teyvat camaraderie. This guide unpacks why Xiangling tops the list—and how other characters stack up on realism, accessibility, and narrative plausibility.

---

### ✅ Key Point 1: Xiangling Is the *Only* Character with Built-In Social Infrastructure  
Unlike adventurers who vanish on quests or scholars buried in archives, Xiangling operates a **public-facing small business**—Wanmin Restaurant—where regular patronage is canonically encouraged and rewarded. Her dialogue confirms she treats customers like friends (“*Ooh! You’re back! Try this new chili oil!*”), invites feedback (“*What’s *your* ideal spice level?*”), and even collaborates with locals (e.g., Chongyun’s exorcism–chili experiments). As a newcomer, you wouldn’t need a Vision, a contract, or divine intervention—just consistent visits, honest opinions, and maybe a willingness to try “Dragon’s Bane” sauce. *Realism score: 9.8/10.*

### ✅ Key Point 2: Realism ≠ Power Level—It’s About Proximity, Personality & Purpose  
The thread revealed a sharp community insight: closeness in Teyvat isn’t earned through combat prowess or world-saving feats—it’s built via **shared routine, mutual interest, and low-barrier interaction**. Characters like Zhongli (archon-level responsibilities), Nahida (Sumeru’s godly duties), or even Bennett (constantly chasing luck) have schedules and stakes that inherently limit casual bonding. Meanwhile, Xiangling’s daily rhythm—cooking, experimenting, chatting with patrons—is *designed* for human-scale connection. Bonus realism points: She’s canonically curious about outsiders (*“Where did you grow up? What spices do they use there?”*) and unbothered by “not being from Teyvat.”

### ✅ Key Point 3: The “Friendship Filter” — 3 Non-Negotiable Criteria for Isekai Plausibility  
Based on top-rated comments across the thread, here’s the unofficial rubric fans use to vet realistic bonds:  
🔹 **Accessibility**: Can you physically find them without a quest trigger? *(Xiangling: Yes—Wanmin Restaurant, open 8 AM–10 PM)*  
🔹 **Relatability**: Do they engage with mundane human experiences? *(Xiangling: Cooking fails, budgeting ingredients, negotiating with Uncle Mao)*  
🔹 **Narrative Permission**: Does lore *invite* outsider integration? *(Xiangling: Yes—her story quests involve mentoring newcomers, and her voice lines treat players as trusted confidants)*  
Other high-scoring candidates? **Collei** (student life + botanical fieldwork), **Klee** (with strict parental supervision but genuine openness), and **Diona** (cat café setting + empathetic listening)—but all require more logistical negotiation than Xiangling’s effortless hospitality.
