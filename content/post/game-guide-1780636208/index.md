### 🔍 Summary  
A viral Reddit post titled *“How did we get here?”*—featuring a surreal, glitchy image of the Oceanid boss mid-battle with distorted UI elements and uncanny lighting—has ignited one of the most unexpectedly profound cultural moments in Genshin Impact’s history. What began as an ironic reaction to the notoriously frustrating 2020 Oceanid fight (a multi-phase, arena-resetting, AI-unpredictable boss) has metastasized into a full-blown meta-narrative: a self-aware, absurdist meme language used by players to express collective fatigue, nostalgia, irony, and even philosophical resignation toward game design, update cycles, and player agency. This guide unpacks how a single boss encounter evolved into a lingua franca—and why it matters far beyond the Abyss.

---

### 💎 Three Key Points  

#### 1. **From Rage-Quit Boss to Ritual Symbol**  
The Oceanid—a water-based, multi-form world boss introduced in Version 1.3—was infamous for its punishing mechanics: sudden phase shifts, invisible hitboxes, terrain-altering summons, and zero visual feedback on stagger or interrupt windows. Players didn’t just *lose*; they felt *erased*. Over time, screenshots of failed attempts—especially those with bizarre glitches (e.g., character models clipping through the arena, UI flickering, or the Oceanid floating mid-air like a glitched deity)—were captioned with the plaintive, existential phrase *“How did we get here?”* It transcended mockery: it became a shared ritual of acknowledgment—like lighting a candle for lost stamina, misplaced Anemo Slimes, and the illusion of control.

#### 2. **Meme as Meta-Critique & Community Compass**  
Unlike fleeting TikTok trends, the *“How did we get here?”* motif functions as a living critique. Players deploy it when:
- A new boss reintroduces Oceanid-style unpredictability (e.g., the Scaramouche fight’s abrupt phase transitions),  
- HoYoverse announces another “quality-of-life” change that inadvertently breaks old strategies,  
- Or even outside the game—when fans jokingly apply it to real-world absurdities (e.g., “How did we get here? Me explaining Geo resonance to my cat”).  
It signals not just frustration, but *historical literacy*: a shorthand proving you’ve survived Genshin’s evolution—and still care enough to joke about it.

#### 3. **The Oceanid Legacy Is Now Canon-Lite**  
HoYoverse hasn’t officially acknowledged the meme—but it *responded*. In Version 4.6’s “Tides of Reverie” event, subtle Easter eggs appeared: a corrupted-looking mural in Fontaine’s underground archives depicting a wave-like entity with glowing, fragmented eyes; dialogue from NPC scholars referencing “the First Drowning” and “cycles without origin”; and a hidden achievement named *“No One Remembers the Beginning”*. While unconfirmed, community sleuths note the timing aligns *exactly* with the Reddit post’s surge. Whether intentional or coincidental, the Oceanid is no longer just content—it’s lore infrastructure. The meme didn’t just reflect culture—it helped *shape* it.
