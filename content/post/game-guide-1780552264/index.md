# 🐎 Why Genshin Impact *Still* Has No Horses in 2024 (Despite Zaibei, Deer, and Empty Stables)

**SEO Title:**  
*Genshin Impact Horses 2024: Why Tevatar Has Stables, Deer, and a Horse-Man—but Zero Rideable Horses (Explained)*

---

### 🔍 Summary  
A viral Reddit post from r/Genshin_Impact—titled *“Why do we not have horses when this thing exists?”*—has reignited one of the game’s longest-running community questions: **Why does Genshin Impact, a world rich with wildlife, lore, and functional infrastructure, still lack even a single horse?** The post highlights the irony of seeing fully modeled, animated deer and elk across Sumeru and Fontaine; encountering Zaibei—the beloved, horse-themed Harbinger whose very identity leans into equine aesthetics; and walking past meticulously detailed, empty stables in Liyue Harbor, Qingce Village, and even the newly added Fontaine equestrian district… all without a single hoofbeat to be heard. This isn’t just about mounts—it’s about environmental storytelling, biome consistency, and the quiet dissonance between worldbuilding ambition and implementation reality.

---

### 🌟 Three Key Points  

#### 1. **The Technical & Art Pipeline Is *Already There*—So What’s Holding It Back?**  
Contrary to early assumptions that “horses are too complex to model,” HoYoverse has already built and deployed multiple quadrupedal, hoofed creature rigs:  
- **Deer** (Sumeru’s forest fauna) and **elk** (Fontaine’s highlands) feature full skeletal animation, terrain-aware locomotion, and dynamic idle behaviors.  
- **Zaibei’s design** includes highly detailed equine anatomy—from mane physics and leg musculature to expressive ear movement—proving HoYoverse *can* render, animate, and optimize horse-like assets at AAA quality.  
Yet no horse NPC or mount exists—even as stable doors swing open to nothing but straw and silence. This suggests the bottleneck isn’t technical capability, but **design priority, systemic integration (e.g., pathfinding, collision, rider interaction), or intentional worldbuilding restraint**—not asset scarcity.

#### 2. **Stables Are Narrative Clues—Not Just Decor**  
Tevatar’s stables aren’t set dressing. They’re *diegetic infrastructure*:  
- Liyue’s bustling harbor stables imply trade caravans and regional transport.  
- Qingce Village’s modest barns suggest agrarian life and livestock dependence.  
- Fontaine’s ornate equestrian academies and statue-lined avenues (including the “Horseman’s Fountain” near Court of Fontaine) reinforce horsemanship as cultural heritage.  
Their emptiness creates a subtle but persistent **lore dissonance**—one players notice precisely *because* Genshin’s environmental storytelling is so strong. As one Redditor put it: *“There are stables all over Tevatar—but not a single horse!!!”* That absence speaks louder than any dialogue line.

#### 3. **It’s Not About “Riding”—It’s About *Presence*, Ecology, and Immersion**  
The original poster clarifies: *“I don’t even wanna ride ‘em—just have them be there for immersion.”* This cuts to the heart of the issue. Players aren’t demanding another mount mechanic (though that’d be welcome). They’re asking for:  
✅ **Ecological coherence** — If deer thrive in forests and elk roam alpine meadows, why wouldn’t feral or domesticated horses inhabit grasslands, coasts, or river valleys?  
✅ **Ambient life** — Grazing horses near Serenitea Pot gardens, startled steeds bolting during Stormterror events, or even docile carriage-pullers in Fontaine’s districts would deepen world density.  
✅ **Cultural texture** — From Liyue’s merchant routes to Fontaine’s dueling traditions and Sumeru’s desert caravans, horses *belong*—not as gameplay tools, but as silent, breathing threads in Tevatar’s tapestry.

---

💡 **Final Thought:**  
HoYoverse has proven it *can* make horses. The question isn’t *“Can they?”*—it’s *“Will they?”* And more intriguingly: *“What does their continued absence tell us about how Tevatar is meant to be experienced—on foot, by glider, and always, deliberately, unmounted?”* Until then, the stables remain open… and the mystery, galloping just out of frame. 🐾
