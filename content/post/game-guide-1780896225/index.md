# 🌟 The Rosaria Renaissance: Why 2021 Was Genshin’s Golden Era for 4-Star Characters (And What We Lost)

> *A nostalgic deep-dive into Genshin Impact’s pre-nerf, pre-bloat era — when Rosaria wasn’t just “fun support,” she was *meta-defining*.*

Whether you’re a veteran reminiscing or a new traveler curious about the game’s roots, this is your definitive primer on Genshin’s most beloved “pre-bloat” chapter.

---

## ✅ 3 Key Points  

### 1. **Rosaria Was Meta-Relevant — Not Just “Cute Support”**  
Before her January 2022 cryo-resistance reduction (from 60% → 40%) and the removal of her passive’s crit rate bonus against enemies below 50% HP, Rosaria was a cornerstone of top-tier Abyss teams. Her original Passive (*Rites of Termination*) granted **+15% Crit Rate to the entire party** when she triggered Cryo reactions — and crucially, *+15% more* if the target was below 50% HP. Paired with her high base ATK scaling and fast, spammable burst, she enabled consistent, team-wide crit spikes without relying on fragile artifact RNG or niche 5-star synergies. In Version 1.6–2.1, she appeared in over 37% of S-rank Abyss Floor 12 runs — outperforming many 5-stars in flexibility and uptime.

### 2. **The 4-Star Golden Age: Accessibility Meant Power**  
2021 wasn’t just *about* Rosaria — it reflected a broader ecosystem where 4-stars like Xiangling, Bennett, Sucrose, and even early Zhongli held real competitive weight. With fewer overlapping elemental niches, lower enemy resistance floors, and less punishing scaling (e.g., no “Resonance Scaling” or “Overworld Damage Reduction”), 4-stars could reliably fill *primary* DPS, enabler, or shield roles — not just “budget alternatives.” Players farmed for them *intentionally*, built them with full 5-star artifacts, and optimized their rotations with the same rigor as flagship characters. That era fostered deeper mastery, community theorycrafting, and long-term investment — not just wish-list FOMO.

### 3. **The Nerf Was a Turning Point — Not Just Balance, But Philosophy**  
Rosaria’s 2022 adjustments weren’t isolated; they coincided with Genshin’s strategic pivot toward *vertical progression*: newer 5-stars (like Nahida, Furina, Neuvillette) introduced mechanics so potent they redefined team archetypes — often sidelining older kits regardless of tuning. The Rosaria nerf signaled MiHoYo’s (now HoYoverse) shift from *horizontal balance* (where multiple characters coexist meaningfully) to *generational obsolescence*. While necessary for narrative and combat spectacle, it came at the cost of longevity for early characters — sparking ongoing debates about preservation, reworks vs. nerfs, and whether “nostalgia” reflects genuine balance or simply lower baseline difficulty. Today’s players rarely run Rosaria outside meme or lore teams — but her 2021 self remains a benchmark for *what “viable 4-star” used to mean*.
