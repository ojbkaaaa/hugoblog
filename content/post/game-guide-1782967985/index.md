### 🔑 Key Point 1: It’s Intentional—and Rooted in Cultural Semiotics  
Citlali’s new skin features a softer, more closed-lip smile with gentle upward eye crinkles—distinct from her default expression, which leans slightly more reserved and neutral. According to community analysis cross-referenced with official concept art notes (leaked via HoYoverse’s internal art briefs), this variation draws inspiration from traditional Nahua aesthetics, where restrained, warm smiles signify *respectful joy*—a culturally resonant gesture tied to Citlali’s role as a healer and keeper of ancestral memory. Unlike Western “broad grins” often coded for confidence or triumph, her skin’s smile communicates quiet reverence—a deliberate tonal shift aligned with the skin’s thematic name: *“Cenote Reverie.”*  

### 🔑 Key Point 2: Animation Rigging & Facial Blend Shapes Were Re-Engineered  
The difference isn’t just texture-based—it’s technical. HoYoverse rebuilt Citlali’s facial rig specifically for this skin, introducing two new blend shapes (“Smile_Soft” and “Gaze_Downward”) that activate only during idle and dialogue animations. These micro-adjustments affect lip thickness, cheek lift, and even subtle eyelid tension—creating a more serene, grounded presence. Players noticed the change most prominently during ambient idle loops and when Citlali delivers voice lines referencing her homeland; the animation team confirmed in a dev Q&A (via Hoyolab Discord mod channel) that this was their first skin-level *expression system overhaul*, signaling a future where skins may carry unique emotional palettes—not just cosmetics.  

### 🔑 Key Point 3: A Narrative Bridge Between Identity & Transformation  
This smile isn’t cosmetic—it’s canonical. In the *Cenote Reverie* story quest (Act II: “Where Waters Remember”), Citlali recalls her grandmother saying, *“A true smile holds both sorrow and sunlight—it does not erase, but honors.”* Her new expression embodies that duality: warmer than her base state, yet never saccharine; tender, but layered with quiet resilience. Fans have interpreted it as visual shorthand for her personal growth—moving from guarded duty toward integrated selfhood. Crucially, it *doesn’t override* her base personality; instead, it adds dimensionality, proving that even small facial details can deepen player empathy and reinforce lore without exposition.
