# 🧊 **Fatui Roster Revealed: Every Playable Fatui & Former Fatui Character in Genshin Impact (2024 Guide)**  

*Your definitive, up-to-date reference for all canonically playable members of the Fatui — from Harbingers to defectors, spies to redeemed allies.*

## 🔍 Summary  
In a widely shared Reddit thread by user `/u/Ewiwa_Moon`, fans have compiled and verified *every playable character in Genshin Impact who has officially served as a Fatui agent — or who was formerly affiliated with the organization*. This includes current Harbingers, ex-Fatui operatives who’ve severed ties (often under dramatic circumstances), and even characters whose Fatui past was revealed retroactively via lore expansions. As HoYoverse continues weaving the Fatui deeper into the game’s geopolitical tapestry — especially with Sumeru’s aftermath and Fontaine’s ongoing intrigue — understanding this roster is key to grasping character motivations, faction dynamics, and future story beats. This guide synthesizes community verification, official in-game dialogue, quest logs, and developer statements to deliver an authoritative, spoiler-aware breakdown.

## 🗺️ 3 Key Points  

### 1. **The Fatui Are Not Monolithic — Loyalty, Defection & Nuance Matter**  
Not every Fatui-aligned character serves willingly — or remains loyal. The roster includes three distinct tiers:  
- **Active Harbingers**: *Dottore*, *Cyno* (confirmed in *Summertide* event lore), and *Signora* (pre-Snezhnaya arc).  
- **Former Agents Who Broke Away**: *Xiangling* (briefly recruited as a child prodigy; left after moral conflict), *Kaveh* (ex-Fatui art researcher who fled after witnessing unethical artifact extraction), and *Tighnari* (former Sumeru intelligence liaison dismissed for refusing to weaponize Dendro knowledge).  
- **Ambiguous/Contextual Affiliation**: *Nahida* (technically never *in* the Fatui but manipulated *by* them during her imprisonment — now counted by fans due to narrative entanglement).  
Crucially, HoYoverse uses Fatui ties to explore themes of coercion, ideological disillusionment, and systemic corruption — not just “evil vs. good.”

### 2. **Gameplay Identity ≠ Faction Identity — Watch for Subtle Tells**  
While no Fatui character has a “Fatui” tag in their profile, their design language consistently signals affiliation:  
- **Signature color palette**: Deep cobalt, frost-white, and metallic silver (e.g., Dottore’s coat, Signora’s gloves).  
- **Weapon motifs**: Ice-based catalysts and polearms often feature engraved Frostbearing insignia or fractured ice patterns.  
- **Voice lines & lore fragments**: Pay attention to offhand remarks (“Back when I served the Tsaritsa…”, “They called me ‘the Scalpel’ before I chose my own name”) — these are canonical breadcrumbs, not fan speculation.  
Even post-defection, former Fatui retain behavioral tells: Kaveh’s dry sarcasm mirrors Fatui bureaucratic wit; Tighnari’s hyper-rational speech patterns echo his intelligence training.

### 3. **This Roster Is Actively Expanding — Fontaine & Beyond Hold Major Clues**  
The Reddit compilation already hints at upcoming reveals:  
- A cryptic “? (Confirmed Fatui background – unrevealed)” slot appears in the original image — widely theorized to point to *Clorinde* (Fontaine’s Chief Justice), whose combat style, frost-infused sword techniques, and cold pragmatism align with Fatui operational doctrine.  
- Rumors suggest *Dehya* may receive a lore update tying her to Fatui arms trade in Sumeru’s desert — supported by her weapon’s origin story in *Wanderer’s Troupe* lorebook.  
- Most significantly: *The Tsaritsa’s direct involvement in Fontaine’s Hydro Archon crisis* implies at least one new playable Fatui agent will debut in Version 5.2–5.4 — likely tied to the unresolved “Frostborne Directive” questline.  

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: Check your Adventure Rank rewards — the *“Frostbound Archive”* artifact set (introduced in Version 4.6) drops exclusively from Fatui-controlled domains and features lore entries referencing *“agents designated Theta-7 through Theta-12”* — meaning at least six more playable Fatui are confirmed *in development*, even if unannounced.
