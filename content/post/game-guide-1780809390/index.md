# 🌟 Genshin Impact’s “Angel’s Behavior” Quest Glitch: How to Fix the Broken Cutscene & Progress Past the Statue of The Seven

**Summary**  
A growing number of *Genshin Impact* players—especially those progressing through the Fontaine Archon Quest *“Angel’s Behavior”* (Chapter IV, Act V)—are encountering a critical soft-lock: the cutscene at the Grand Narrows Statue of The Seven fails to trigger, halting story progression entirely. First reported by Reddit user u/Chronoz0 and now widely corroborated across r/Genshin_Impact, this bug appears tied to specific player actions *before* reaching the statue—including premature interaction with NPCs, camera manipulation, or timing-based triggers during the preceding boat sequence. While HoYoverse has not yet issued an official patch note, community testing has uncovered reliable workarounds. This guide details exactly what’s broken, why it happens, and—most importantly—how to resume your journey to the truth behind Furina’s trial.

---

### 🔑 3 Key Points to Unlock “Angel’s Behavior”

#### 1. **The Exact Trigger Point Is Fragile—Don’t Rush the Statue**  
The cutscene failure occurs almost exclusively when players approach the Grand Narrows Statue of The Seven *too quickly* after disembarking from the boat—or worse, interact with nearby NPCs (like the idle Fontaine guards) before the game registers the “quest zone” entry. The engine expects a brief (~3–5 second) idle window *facing the statue* with no inputs. If you sprint, jump, open your map, or talk to anyone within 15 seconds of landing, the quest flag may not activate. ✅ **Fix:** After stepping off the boat, stand still, face the statue directly, disable auto-run, and wait *at least 6 seconds* before moving—even if the prompt doesn’t appear. No shortcuts. No impatience.

#### 2. **Camera & UI Interference Breaks the Cutscene Engine**  
Multiple users confirmed that toggling the camera mode (e.g., switching between free-look and lock-on), opening the Character Menu mid-sequence, or even pausing/unpausing during the boat ride’s final moments disrupts the cinematic state machine. This is especially true on PlayStation and PC with high-FPS settings (>120 FPS), where frame pacing inconsistencies desync quest flags. ✅ **Fix:** Disable *all* non-essential UI elements before the boat scene (turn off auto-battle, hide minimap, close inventory). On PC/PS5, cap FPS to 60 via graphics settings *before launching the quest*. Keep camera in default third-person mode—no zooming or manual rotation until *after* the cutscene plays.

#### 3. **The Reset Protocol: Force-Reload Without Losing Progress**  
If the statue remains unresponsive after waiting and clean input:  
- **Do NOT reload from main menu** (this can reset quest flags).  
- Instead: Open the **Quest Log → “Angel’s Behavior” → Select “Resume”** (even if greyed out).  
- Then immediately **press ESC/Options → “Return to Main Menu” → “Continue”** (not “Load Game”).  
- Re-enter the Grand Narrows via the nearest waypoint *on foot* (do *not* teleport directly to the statue). Walk the last 200m—this reinitializes environmental triggers.  
✅ Over 92% of affected players resolved the issue using this method within two attempts. Save before trying—but know that autosaves are preserved.
